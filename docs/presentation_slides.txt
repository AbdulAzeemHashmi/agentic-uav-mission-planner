SLIDE 2: Problem Statement
- UAV mission planning requires strict coordinate bounds.
- Manual flight planning errors lead to:
  * Entering no-fly zone airspace regulations.
  * Over-estimating battery capacity limits, leading to crashes.
  * Overlooking ceiling limits (e.g. legal 80m ceiling).
  * Incomplete commands (missing takeoff or RTL).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 3: Project Objectives
- Create an automated, conversational agent-based planner.
- Support natural language processing to extract flight goals.
- Enforce 7 core compliance rules (airspace rules, battery limits, leg distance).
- Visualize interactive route polylines and restricted zones on Folium maps.
- Export standard QGroundControl .plan files for direct autopilot execution.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 4: System Architecture & Multi-Agent Flow
- User Input (NLP/Form) -> Streamlit Panel
- Mission Understanding Agent (Regex & Gemini 1.5 Flash parser)
- Waypoint Planner Agent (Square, Grid scanning, Circle orbit, Perimeter)
- Safety Compliance Agent (Point & Segment checks, metric calculations)
- Correction Agent (Shapely boundary normal shift engine)
- Report Agent & Exporter (PDF manual, CSV table, JSON, QGC plans)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 5: Physics-Based Battery Estimation
- Real-world drones do not consume energy linearly.
- SkyGuard AI calculates separate flight power phases:
  * Climb: 215W at 4m/s
  * Cruise: 120W at 10m/s
  * Descent: 115W at 2m/s
  * Hover: 130W
- Captures takeoff ascent, cruise distance, landing descent, and waiting hover times against a 90 Wh capacity standard.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 6: Advanced Geofencing Corrections
- Waypoint point-checks are insufficient; flight line segments can cut restricted boundaries.
- Segments are projected locally to metric flat Cartesian coordinates (eliminating coordinate degree mismatch).
- Waypoints inside polygons are shifted along the Shapely boundary outward normal vector by exactly 15m.
- Waypoints inside circles are shifted along the radial normal vector from the center.
